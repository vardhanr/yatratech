Tipping

Tipping is quite common in France and it's good etiquette to leave one. How much money you leave depends on the service and how happy you were with it. Receipts always include a service charge.

Expected to tip

Internet

Internet speed in France is on average 8.9 Mbps which is 47% slower than in Switzerland (16.7 Mbps).

WiFi coverage in France is very high. Most hotels, hostels, cafes, restaurants and bars have it.

ATMs

Automatic teller machines (local names: distributeur or distributeurs automatiques de billets (DAB)) are very common in France. You can find them in both large & small cities, tourist areas etc.

You do not need a chip & PIN card to use an ATM — your standard magnetic card will work fine.

Very common

Driving And Traffic Rules

In France you drive on the right side. Most cars have manual transmission (stick).

Unless otherwise posted, the speed limits for cars and motorcycles are as follows: 110 km/h (68 mph) on expressways, 130 km/h (80 mph) on motorways, 30 km/h (18 mph) in residential areas, 50 km/h (31 mph) in all built-up areas and 90 km/h (55 mph) on undivided highways.

Right turn on red lights is strictly forbidden unless an additional green semaphore arrow allows it.

It is illegal to drive with blood alcohol content higher than 0.05%.

Mobile phone

You can buy a SIM card for about €10.71 (12.53 CHF), which includes 2 GB of data plan. You can choose from 4 telecoms: Bouygues Télécom, Free, Orange or SFR.

France uses similar GSM mobile networks (GSM 1800 and GSM 900) as Switzerland so you should be able to normally use your mobile phone.

If you don't want to buy SIM card in France upon arrival, make sure to check the roaming charges with your telecom provider before your departure so there are no surprises when you return home.

Credit & debit cards

Credit cards are widely accepted within France (at hotels, shops, and restaurants, travel agencies, car-rental agencies etc.). Visa, MasterCard and Maestro are the most widely accepted. American Express is less common. Diners Club is pretty rare. Discover, JCB, UnionPay and RuPay are unknown.

Widely accepted

Sockets & plugs

France uses 230V, 50Hz with sockets and plugs Type C and Type E.

No adapter or transformer needed

Dial code

To call France from Switzerland, dial + or 00 (exit code for Switzerland), then 33 (the country code for France), then the area code (without the initial 0) and the local number.

01 1234567

+ 33 1 1234567

For local calls within France, start with the area code (with the initial 0). In the case above area code is 1 .
